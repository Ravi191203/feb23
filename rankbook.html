James Gosling

James Gosling is a Canadian computer scientist, best known as the father of the Java programming language.

He was born in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, on May 19, 1955.

Gosling received his bachelor's degree in computer science from the University of Calgary in 1977.

He went on to earn his master's degree and Ph.D. in computer science from Carnegie Mellon University in 1983.

Gosling joined Sun Microsystems in 1984, where he led the team that developed Java.

Java was released in 1995 and quickly became one of the most popular programming languages in the world.

Gosling left Sun Microsystems in 2000 and joined Google in 2005.

He is currently a vice president at Google and is working on the development of new programming languages.
